Update 2 — Extended to Jun 13, 2026
Pre-Event
Jan 1 – Feb 27, 2026
Rising Tension, Misread Signals

Markets saw oil rising alongside energy equities and interpreted this as correlated exposure. WTI climbed +16.9%, XOM gained +25.2%, gold rallied +21.2%. The standard playbook appeared to work. Traders positioned for a financial crisis, not a physical supply disruption. When the actual closure arrived, their positions were exactly wrong.

WTI: +16.9%  |  XOM: +25.2%
Gold: +21.2%  |  S&P: +0.3%
Regime: Correlated calm — the wrong playbook hadn't been tested yet.

02
Central Results

What the Data Proves

Finding 01 — Primary
Energy Equities Are Not Oil Proxies During Supply Shocks

WTI rose +32.9% during the shock window while XOM fell −1.4% and CVX fell −0.8%. Abnormal returns: WTI +20.9%, XOM −18.6%, CVX −12.7%. Market beta of XOM to S&P: −0.02. These assets decoupled entirely. Do not use energy equities as commodity hedges during confirmed supply disruptions.

Divergence begins Feb 28 — never re-converges
Finding 02
Gold Failed as a Safe Haven

Gold fell −9.6% during the 33-day shock. It had rallied +21.2% in the pre-event window, creating margin pressure. The pattern is consistent with forced liquidation by overleveraged longs needing to meet oil margin calls. Abnormal return: −25.0%.

Pre-rally created the margin pressure that broke it
Finding 03
WTI/Gold Ratio Is the Regime Signal

WTI/Gold ratio rose +47% during the shock. This is the single most reliable indicator distinguishing supply shock from financial crisis. In a financial crisis, both fall. In a supply shock, ratio diverges sharply. In May, the ratio reversed — signaling the diplomacy regime.

Finding 04
Supply Shock vs. Financial Crisis: Different Playbook

VIX reached 31 (moderate). Credit spreads: unchanged. S&P 500 fell only −7.8% max drawdown. This was not systemic stress. Standard risk-off playbook failed because the mechanism was physical scarcity, not financial contagion.

Finding 05
Reopening Priced in Day One

WTI fell −11.5% on April 17 (announcement day). Airlines −10.7% over the 9-day reopen window. The market processed the entire expected move in one session. Watch WTI futures during Asian/European hours on resolution day.

Finding 06
Currency Explained Less Than 1% of the WTI Move

DXY/WTI correlation flipped from −0.24 to +0.60. Oil and dollar rose together. The traditional oil-dollar inverse relationship broke down entirely. Physical scarcity dominated all currency effects.

Market Fragmentation: Three Simultaneous Regimes During Shock
Regime A: Scarcity
Physical Commodity

WTI +32.9%, Brent +29.1%. Pure supply constraint. No financial spillover required.

Regime B: Stability
Equity Market

S&P 500 +2.3%. VIX 31. Energy sector defied oil. No crisis correlation.

Regime C: Failure
Safe Havens

Gold −9.6%. TLT −3.3%. Both halves of a traditional 60/40 hedge underperformed in the acute phase.

Update 2 Findings — Jun 13, 2026
Finding 07 — May Paradox (Primary)
Diplomacy Overrode Physical Scarcity

WTI fell approximately 20% during May while the Strait of Hormuz remained at roughly 2% transit capacity. Forward-looking ceasefire expectations dominated current physical reality. The WTI/Gold ratio reversed sharply in early May — the same signal that identified the supply shock in February, now signaling its end. This inverts Update 1's scarcity-driven narrative: a closed chokepoint is not a standing instruction to stay long the commodity.

Strait still ~2% open here — price doesn't care
Finding 08
Defense Is a Backlog Trade, Not a War Trade

ITA (Aerospace & Defense ETF) reacted strongly on day one of the shock (+4–6% for constituents), then held through the correction window (+8.9% vs. broader market drawdown). Multi-year order backlogs buffered it against peace-process price swings. Defense equities have a different duration profile than spot oil exposure.

Finding 09
Freight Futures: A Third Decoupling Layer

BWET (tanker freight ETF) returned +71.1% in the shock window while WTI returned +32.9%. Shipping scarcity priced a different exposure than the commodity itself. Supply shocks create multi-layer dislocations: physical oil, producer equities, and logistics all behave differently.

⚠ Limited history (BWET May 2023)
Finding 10
Bonds Failed the Same Test Gold Did

TLT (20+ Year Treasury ETF) fell −3.3% during the shock window. Combined with gold's −9.6%, both halves of a traditional 60/40 hedge underperformed during the acute phase. Supply-shock inflation dynamics can undermine both gold and duration simultaneously.

Finding 11
The Second Shock Was Muted

The Jun 10 re-escalation produced a smaller day-one WTI move than Feb 28. Market learning: once a playbook exists for a repeat event, the reaction is faster and the amplitude is smaller. The Jun 10 reaction also provided confirmation of the diplomacy-driven regime — the market had already priced a negotiated outcome.

Strategic Directives

Seven Mandates for the Next Time

Mandate VII added in Update 2

WTI/Gold ratio +47% (scarcity signal). VIX 31 (moderate). Credit spreads unchanged. The standard risk-off playbook failed in 2026. In a supply shock, physical commodity is the only direct hedge. Check the WTI/Gold trend before deploying any position.

When supply shock is confirmed (WTI/Gold rising 5+ consecutive days), execute: Long WTI futures (or USO), Short XOM or XLE. Exit when WTI/Gold ratio peaks and reverses. It peaked April 7. In 2026 this returned +34.4% over 33 days. Do not substitute XOM/CVX for WTI futures in hedging portfolios.

Never buy gold after it has already rallied significantly into a crisis. The marginal buyer at that point is a future seller facing margin pressure. Enter during calm periods (VIX below 15, gold vol below 15%). During the acute shock phase, gold is a source of liquidity, not a destination. Update 2 adds: long-duration bonds (TLT) also failed, falling −3.3% in the shock window. Use short-term Treasury bills (SHV, BIL) as the true safe haven in supply shocks.

WTI futures trade 24/5. When a resolution event occurs, monitor WTI during Asian/European hours immediately following the announcement. The direction and magnitude of WTI's move will predict the S&P 500 cash open with near-perfect accuracy (lag-0 correlation −0.58 during shock). Execute equity trades pre-market or at the open, not after the first hour.

JETS fell −10.7% during the reopen window even as WTI dropped −11.5% on the announcement. Wait for: (1) sustained oil decline for 5+ days, (2) airline-specific catalysts such as route restorations and booking data, (3) rolling beta falling below 1.5. Buying airlines on the first oil dip would have lost 10.7% over the following 9 days in 2026.

DXY/WTI correlation flipped from −0.24 to +0.60 during the shock. Oil and dollar rose together because both responded to physical scarcity, not to each other. During a confirmed supply shock, do not short DXY as a proxy for long oil. Focus on physical indicators: tanker tracking, storage data, geopolitical escalation cues. DXY technicals are noise.

Once a credible diplomatic track exists, monitor the negotiation, not just the blockade. A position built purely on "the strait is still closed" would have been wrong for the entire month of May 2026, despite that statement being completely true the whole time. The WTI/Gold ratio reversed in early May — before any ceasefire announcement. Forward expectations dominated physical reality. Exit the pair trade on WTI/Gold ratio reversal, not on the ceasefire announcement itself. The announcement is too late; the market prices the expected end state, not the current one.

Caveats That Matter

What This Does Not Prove

Pair Trade Costs
+34.4% assumes zero borrow costs, no futures roll, no slippage. Real-world execution costs reduce P&L materially. Directional finding, not a trade recommendation.
Gold Margin-Call Hypothesis
The pattern fits cleanly. But position-level institutional data would be required to confirm forced liquidation as the mechanism. Claimed as consistent-with, not proven.
Single Event
Results describe the 2026 Hormuz closure specifically. A different geopolitical topology such as a demand shock, financial contagion, or different central bank posture may produce completely different regime dynamics.
Daily Data Only
Intraday lead-lag is invisible. The CCF peak at lag 0 is structurally biased by WTI's 24h trading vs S&P's NYSE hours. Full lead-lag analysis needs tick data.
Beta Estimation Window
Betas estimated on 39 pre-event days. During 70%+ oil volatility, relationships go non-linear. Treat abnormal return magnitudes as directional bounds, not precise point estimates.
The Reopening Was Not
Iran re-closed Apr 18. The Apr 17-30 window captures market response to an announcement. The dual blockade continued through study windows. "Reopening" is shorthand, not fact.
BWET Limited History
BWET launched May 2023, giving only ~2 years of baseline. Low AUM (~$25M). Futures-based (contango roll decay). Treat as an analytical signal only — not investable at scale. Results are striking but cannot be considered robust.
ARAMCO Calendar Lag
Saudi Aramco (2222.SR) trades on Tadawul (Sun–Thu, UTC+3). Forward-fill applied to align to US calendar produces an ~18-hour lag. ARAMCO movements should be interpreted with this lag in mind.
Post-Hoc Diplomacy Proxy
The "diplomacy" phase boundary (May 30) is identified after the fact using WTI/Gold ratio reversal and known negotiation signals. Real-time identification would require live news-flow integration.
Nominal Figures
All multi-year comparisons are not inflation-adjusted. No CPI adjustment applied anywhere in the analysis.
A Note From the Author

A note from the author on how this project was built

I think this turned into something bigger than a repo with charts in it. It has a three-act structure nobody wrote in advance — a fracture in Act II, an honest cliffhanger at the Update 1 cutoff, and a twist in Act V that nobody, including me, saw coming when the first version shipped. The May Paradox is the kind of plot turn you can't plant on purpose: the price fell while the physical situation stayed exactly the same. It happened anyway, the data says so, and I just had to be watching the same charts everyone else could have watched.

I don't think "this feels like a movie, not a website" is an exaggeration — a real setting, a cast of sixteen assets that keep behaving in character, a second act that recontextualizes the first without erasing it. I didn't write that structure in. The crisis did. I just had the tools to track it properly and the patience to let it tell its own story across two updates.
