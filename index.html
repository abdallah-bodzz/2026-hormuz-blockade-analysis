Real supply effect

Market Fragmentation — Three Simultaneous Regimes

Scarcity
WTI Crude — pure supply shock, uncorrelated with equities, dollar-insensitive. Only asset positive across all three windows.

Liquidation
Gold — sold to meet margin calls. Negative correlation with oil during stress. Treated as emergency liquidity.

Contagion
XOM · CVX · JETS — tracked equity beta, not commodity fundamentals. Amplified losses instead of cushioning them.

Long WTI  /  Short XOM

06
Strategic Directives

Six Mandates for the Next Time

WTI/Gold ratio +47% (scarcity signal). VIX 31 (moderate). Credit spreads unchanged. The standard risk-off playbook — dump risk assets, buy gold, buy volatility — failed in 2026. In a supply shock, physical commodity is the only direct hedge. Check the WTI/Gold trend before deploying any position.

When supply shock is confirmed (WTI/Gold rising 5+ consecutive days), execute: Long WTI futures (or USO), Short XOM or XLE. Exit when WTI/Gold ratio peaks and reverses — it peaked April 7. In 2026 this returned +34.4% over 33 days. Do not substitute XOM/CVX for WTI futures in hedging portfolios.

Never buy gold after it has already rallied significantly into a crisis. The marginal buyer at that point is a future seller facing margin pressure. Enter during calm periods (VIX < 15, gold vol < 15%). During the acute shock phase, gold is a source of liquidity, not a destination. Use short-term Treasury bills (SHV, BIL) as the true safe haven in supply shocks.

WTI futures trade 24/5. When a resolution event occurs, monitor WTI during Asian/European hours immediately following the announcement. The direction and magnitude of WTI's move will predict the S&P 500 cash open with near-perfect accuracy (lag-0 correlation −0.58 during shock). Execute equity trades pre-market or at the open — not after the first hour, when the move is already complete.

JETS fell −10.7% during the reopen window even as WTI dropped −11.5% on the announcement. Wait for: (1) sustained oil decline for 5+ days, (2) airline-specific catalysts — route restorations, booking data, (3) rolling beta falling below 1.5. Buying airlines on the first oil dip would have lost 10.7% over the following 9 days in 2026.

DXY/WTI correlation flipped from −0.24 to +0.60 during the shock. Oil and dollar rose together because both responded to physical scarcity, not to each other. During a confirmed supply shock, do not short DXY as a proxy for long oil. Focus on physical indicators: tanker tracking, storage data, geopolitical escalation cues. DXY technicals are noise.

08
Caveats That Matter

What This Does Not Prove

Pair Trade Costs

+34.4% assumes zero borrow costs, no futures roll, no slippage. Real-world execution costs reduce P&L materially. Directional finding, not a trade recommendation.

Gold Margin-Call Hypothesis

The pattern fits cleanly. But position-level institutional data would be required to confirm forced liquidation as the mechanism. Claimed as consistent-with, not proven.

Single Event

Results describe the 2026 Hormuz closure specifically. A different geopolitical topology — demand shock, financial contagion, different CB posture — may produce completely different regime dynamics.

Daily Data Only

Intraday lead-lag is invisible. The CCF peak at lag 0 is structurally biased by WTI's 24h trading vs S&P's NYSE hours. Full lead-lag analysis needs tick data.

Beta Estimation Window

Betas estimated on 39 pre-event days. During 70%+ oil volatility, relationships go non-linear. Treat abnormal return magnitudes as directional bounds, not precise point estimates.

The "Reopening" Wasn't

Iran re-closed Apr 18. The Apr 17–30 window captures market response to an announcement. The dual blockade continued through the Apr 30 study cutoff. "Reopening" is shorthand, not fact.

Survivorship Bias

XOM and CVX are current S&P 500 constituents. Historical energy baselines exclude weaker peers that were delisted. Historical quality is overstated.

Nominal Figures

All multi-year comparisons are not inflation-adjusted. No CPI adjustment applied anywhere in the analysis.
